Banded Archerfish

The Banded Archerfish is unique from other fish in the fact it shoots projectiles, hence its name.

Through eons of evolutionary changed, the archerfish have adapted to spew jet waves at a distance, usually 150cm (or 59 inches), but much larger fish can spit as far as 2-3 meters (6-9 feet). This is their prefered method of catching and eating prey

They can even teach each other shoot down moving targets! With an accuracy of 50%. This may not sound accurate, but remember, a baseball player only needs to hit 1 in 3 pitches (33.3%) to be a superstar!

Moon Jelly

This may not look it, but this is actually a plankton

Well... technically. Jellyfish are simple creatures, and are considered to be zooplankton for their soft, transparent bodies that are too weak to swim against a current

The moon jelly in particular has hundreds of fine tentacles and a weak sting opposed to the longer, lesser tentacles of other jellyfish.

This was the first jellyfish species I personally documented in 1997, I mistakenly documented others as moon jellies but luckily a colleague corrected me

Common Bottlenose Dolphin

Bottlenose Dolphins are common in tropical and temperate oceans, can swim up to 30 kilometers and hour and dive as deep as 250 meters into the water. I love dolphins, they are very intelligent, but they can also be very evil and many of the things I have seen on the job are not appropriate for this blog. But many of their good qualities can be seen in my Dolphin sub blog.

The formation of their beaks make it seem as though they are always smiling, but this is just a case of anthropomorphism, where we assign human emotions to animals. Similarly to that video I see of someone "Tickling" a sting ray while it's gasping for life.

It may look like it's smiling, but imagine being deep underwater and being tickled, forcing what little air you have out of your lungs and flooding it with sea water. In my delinquent days, I would get into fights and many times I angered the wrong man, I can liken the experience of dealing with the consequences of my actions to these fish. Like I am seconds away from drowning, only to be dragged deeper and deeper into the ocean. This is why empathy is a very important thing for someone to have. As much as I like them, in my experience, Dolphins lack empathy.

Red Lionfish

The Lionfish is native to the Indo-Pacific region of the Red Sea. But I have seen many in the Western Atlantic Basin, particularly when I traveled to Jamaica to document marine populations there in 2003

They're an invasive species in the western hemisphere, they're so numerous at this point that it is pointless to get rid of them. They are here to stay, and while we have a responsibility to keep them in check, natural selection will have the final say in the coming centuries, hopefully it creates a balance and does not result in the extinction of populations unable to defend themselves from it.

Some people in Jamaica have taken to eating lionfish, as it has no predators there. I joined in if it was ever served where I was at, I personally dislike the taste, but I'm happy to know I contributed to nature's attempt at finding balance for Atlantic Basin in these strange times, however miniscule.

Galapagos Penguin

The Galapagos Penguin live northmost of all other penguin species, generally, the western part of the Galapagos islands. They are also the smallest of them all.. They can be distinguished by a narrow C-shaped band of white feathers on both sides of their heads from their eyes to their chin, and a single band of black feathers across their breast

They hunt in groups, mainly eating smaller fish like pilchards and mullets, and sometimes crustaceans.

They're threatened by invasive predators in the form of land mammals such as dogs, cats, and rats, as well as el nino events which greatly reduce their access to food. They are currently endagered, with only 1,200 individuals alive at the moment.

Egypt - 1988

In October 1988, my mother fell gravely ill. In order to arrange her treatment, my grandfather (Bottom right) flew me and a few others we met shortly before departure from Tokyo to Cairo, Egypt, where doctors could treat her illness.

Unfortunately, our plane crashed near Hong Kong, we survived, forcing us to walk and drive there over the course of two months. We traveled through Hong Kong, Singapore, India, Pakistan, The United Arab Emirates, and the Red Sea before finally reaching Egypt.

I (Top left) was a juvenile delinquent back then, and while I did and still do love my mother, during the trip I gave them all a very hard time. Looking back, I did enjoy the trip. Unfortunately, two of us passed away on our way back. I still miss them...
